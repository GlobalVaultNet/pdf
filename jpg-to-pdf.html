How to Convert Images to PDF

Converting images to PDF format is a common need for document management, archiving, and sharing. GlobalVaultNet PDF's JPG to PDF converter provides a simple, secure way to transform your images into professional PDF documents without uploading files to external servers.

Our conversion tool uses the pdf-lib JavaScript library to create PDF documents directly in your browser. When you select images, they are processed locally and embedded into a new PDF document with options for page sizing and orientation. The entire process happens on your device, ensuring your images remain private and secure.

Supported Image Formats

Our converter supports all major image formats commonly used today:

  • JPEG/JPG: The most common format for photographs and web images. Excellent compression with good quality.
  • PNG: Ideal for images with transparency or sharp edges like screenshots and graphics.
  • GIF: Supports simple animations and transparency. Static frames are extracted for PDF conversion.
  • BMP: Uncompressed bitmap format often used in Windows environments.
  • WEBP: Modern format with superior compression, increasingly used on the web.

Page Size and Orientation Options

Our tool offers flexible options for how images are placed in the PDF:

  • Fit to Image: Each PDF page matches the dimensions of the source image. This preserves the original aspect ratio and resolution.
  • A4: Standard international paper size (210 x 297 mm). Images are scaled to fit within A4 dimensions.
  • Letter: Common North American paper size (8.5 x 11 inches). Suitable for US business documents.
  • Legal: Longer North American format (8.5 x 14 inches) often used for contracts.

Common Use Cases

Converting images to PDF serves numerous practical purposes:

  • Document Scanning: Convert photos of documents into professional PDF files for archiving or sharing.
  • Photo Albums: Create PDF photo albums from collections of images for easy distribution.
  • Receipt Management: Organize expense receipts by converting them to a single PDF for expense reports.
  • Portfolio Creation: Compile design work or photography into a PDF portfolio.
  • Form Submission: Convert signed documents or ID photos to PDF for online form submissions.

Quality Preservation

Our conversion process maintains the original image quality. Images are embedded into the PDF at their original resolution, ensuring no loss of detail. The resulting PDF can be viewed, printed, or shared while preserving the visual fidelity of your source images.

Security and Privacy

As with all GlobalVaultNet PDF tools, your images are processed entirely within your browser. No data is transmitted to our servers, ensuring complete privacy. This makes our tool ideal for converting sensitive documents, personal photos, or confidential images.

Frequently Asked Questions

What image formats can I convert to PDF?

Our tool supports JPG, JPEG, PNG, GIF, BMP, and WEBP formats. You can convert single images or combine multiple images into one PDF document.

Can I convert multiple images to one PDF?

Yes, you can select multiple images and they will be combined into a single PDF document. Each image becomes one page in the resulting PDF.

Will the image quality be preserved?

Yes, images are embedded at their original resolution. There is no quality loss during conversion.

Are my images uploaded to your servers?

No, all conversion happens in your browser. Your images never leave your device or touch our servers.

Can I rearrange images before converting?

Currently, images are converted in the order they were selected. For specific ordering, select images in your desired sequence.

Is there a limit to how many images I can convert?

There's no fixed limit. The practical limit depends on your device's available memory. Most users can convert 20-50 images at once.

Can I convert images with transparency?

Yes, PNG images with transparency are supported. The transparency will be preserved in the PDF where possible.

What page size should I choose?

'Fit to Image' is recommended for most cases as it preserves the original image dimensions. Choose A4 or Letter if you need standard paper sizes for printing.
